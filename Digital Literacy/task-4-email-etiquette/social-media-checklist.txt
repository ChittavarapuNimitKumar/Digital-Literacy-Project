Do’s:
1.Audit Privacy Settings: Regularly check your account settings to control who can view your personal information and posts.
2.Professional Networking: Use platforms like LinkedIn to connect with industry professionals and alumni.
3.Showcase Achievements: Share updates about your academic projects, internships, and volunteer work to build a positive brand.
4.Fact-Check Content: Verify the accuracy of information before sharing to avoid spreading misinformation.
5.Professional Imagery: Use a clean, professional profile picture for any accounts that might be visible to recruiters. 
Don’ts:
1.Impulsive Posting: Avoid posting when you are emotional, as digital footprints are often permanent.
2.Share Sensitive Info: Never post private details like your student ID, home address, or full class schedule.
3.Engage with Trolls: Stay away from online arguments or toxic behavior that could reflect poorly on your character.
4.Publicly Complain: Do not post negative comments about professors, your university, or your workplace.
5.Post Conduct Violations: Avoid sharing any content that depicts illegal activities or violates your university’s code of conduct.
